# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

DevConnect exists to connect IT professionals. For that to work, we need a community where every person — regardless of experience level, background, or identity — feels safe to participate, ask questions, share ideas, and grow.

We as contributors, maintainers, and community members pledge to make participation in DevConnect a harassment-free experience for everyone. We commit to treating all people with dignity and respect, and to building a space where the best ideas win, not the loudest voices.

-----

## Our Standards

### ✅ Behaviors we encourage

- **Being welcoming** — greet newcomers warmly; everyone was a beginner once.
- **Being respectful** — disagree on ideas, not on people. Critique code, not character.
- **Being constructive** — feedback should help someone improve, not discourage them from trying.
- **Being patient** — not everyone communicates the same way or at the same speed.
- **Being collaborative** — share knowledge freely; the project improves when everyone learns.
- **Being honest** — give accurate information, admit when you don't know something, and acknowledge mistakes.
- **Giving credit** — recognize the work of others, both in code and in discussion.

### ❌ Behaviors we do not tolerate

- **Harassment** — any form of unwanted, offensive, or threatening behavior toward any person.
- **Discrimination** — hostile or exclusionary treatment based on age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.
- **Insults and personal attacks** — name-calling, belittling, or dismissing someone's contributions based on who they are rather than the merit of the work.
- **Sexual content or attention** — sharing sexually explicit material or making unwanted sexual advances in any project space.
- **Trolling and bad faith** — deliberately derailing conversations, spreading misinformation, or engaging in provocative behavior with no intent to contribute constructively.
- **Doxxing** — publishing or threatening to publish someone's private information without their consent.
- **Retaliation** — taking action against someone for reporting a violation or supporting someone who has.

-----

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all spaces managed by the DevConnect project, including but not limited to:

- GitHub repository (issues, pull requests, discussions, code reviews, commit messages)
- Project documentation and wikis
- Community chat channels (Discord)
- Social media accounts representing the project
- In-person or virtual events associated with the project

It also applies when an individual is representing the project in public spaces. For example, using the official project email address, posting from an official social media account, or acting as a designated representative at an event.

-----

## Enforcement

### Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it promptly. You can do so by:

- **Email:** `conductdevconnect@m41k80dev.com` — reaches the maintainer team directly and confidentially.
- **GitHub:** Use GitHub's private reporting feature on any issue or PR if the violation occurred there.
- **Discord:** Direct message a moderator in the [DevConnect Discord](https://discord.gg/5xEWnfJDjt).

When making a report, please include:

- Your contact information (so we can follow up with you)
- Names or usernames of anyone involved
- A description of the incident and where it occurred
- Any screenshots, links, or other evidence if available
- Any additional context you think is relevant

All reports are treated confidentially. The reporting person's identity will not be disclosed to anyone outside the core maintainer team without explicit consent.

### What happens after a report

1. **Acknowledgment** — The maintainer team will acknowledge receipt of your report within **48 hours**.
1. **Review** — Maintainers will review the report, gather any additional context, and discuss the appropriate response.
1. **Decision** — A decision will be communicated to the reporter within **7 days** of the initial acknowledgment.
1. **Action** — The appropriate enforcement action will be applied (see below).

We are committed to acting consistently and fairly. Maintainers who are involved in a reported incident will recuse themselves from that decision.

### Enforcement guidelines

Responses will be proportional to the severity and context of the violation:

#### 1. Correction

**Applies to:** Minor violations — an unintentionally offensive comment, a lapse in tone, or a first-time infraction.

**Action:** A private written note from a maintainer explaining why the behavior was problematic and what a more appropriate response would have looked like. No public record.

-----

#### 2. Warning

**Applies to:** A more serious or repeated minor violation.

**Action:** A formal written warning with a clear statement of what behavior must stop. A period of restricted interaction may be applied (e.g., no comment on certain issues for a set time). Continued violations escalate to a temporary ban.

-----

#### 3. Temporary Ban

**Applies to:** A significant violation of community standards, sustained inappropriate behavior, or continued behavior after a warning.

**Action:** A temporary suspension from all project spaces for a defined period (typically 7–30 days). No interaction with the community or maintainers during this period. Return is conditional on a written acknowledgment of the violation.

-----

#### 4. Permanent Ban

**Applies to:** Severe violations — harassment, threats, doxxing, discrimination, sustained predatory behavior, or repeated serious violations.

**Action:** Permanent removal from all DevConnect community spaces. This decision is final and not subject to appeal in most cases.

-----

## Appeals

If you believe an enforcement decision was made in error, you may appeal by emailing `conductdevconnect@m41k80dev.com` with the subject line `Appeal: [brief description]`. Appeals will be reviewed by a maintainer not involved in the original decision. The outcome of an appeal is final.

## A final word

Rules exist to protect people, not to police them. We trust our community to engage in good faith. If you ever feel unsure about whether something crosses a line, ask yourself: *"Would I be comfortable if my message appeared on the project's public README?"*

When in doubt, lead with kindness. 💜
